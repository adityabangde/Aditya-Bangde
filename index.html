Mind Speak

A Wearable EEG-Based Interface for Real-Time Conversion of Brain Signals to Synthesised Speech

What it does

Our system enables individuals who are unable to speak to communicate using only their thoughts.

By wearing a lightweight EEG headset, brain signals associated with imagined speech are captured and converted into actual spoken words using machine learning and a text-to-speech engine.

This offers a real-time, non-invasive, and portable solution for people with conditions like ALS, paralysis, or stroke.

Importantly, it is a low-cost, DIY system , designed to be accessible to students and researchers , encouraging more hands-on exploration and innovation in a field where research is currently limited due to the high cost of commercial EEG devices.

How It Works

  • We started by identifying that imagined speech EEG signals primarily lie between 5 Hz and 20 Hz. This insight shaped our hardware choices for optimal signal capture.

  • To amplify microvolt-level EEG signals, we used the AD620 instrumentation amplifier with 1000x gain and a 90 dB CMRR, ensuring clean signal extraction and noise suppression.

  • Environmental noise was minimized using twisted-pair wiring, a low-pass filter on the power supply, and plans for coaxial cables and per-electrode notch filters in future iterations.

  • We used a 32-bit ADS1263 ADC sampling at 400 Hz with a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W for high-resolution, real-time signal acquisition and edge-level preprocessing.

  • To validate the system, we trained a CNN-LSTM model on the Emotiv Epoc X dataset. The model featured 3 convolutional layers and 2 LSTM layers, achieving 98.46% training accuracy in classifying imagined digits.

  • Next steps include integrating per-channel filtering, dedicated microcontrollers, and enhancing model accuracy with larger, more diverse datasets. Our goal is to build a fully modular, low-cost, and open-source EEG-to-speech platform.

Hardware Stack

  • EEG Headset (3D-Printed & Modular)

    Built using an open-source outer shell, this headset features custom-designed electrode holders and a dual screw mechanism for comfort and precise electrode placement. Designed around the 10–20 system, it is lightweight, adjustable, and cost-efficient—ideal for research and development environments.

  • Electrodes (8 Channels)

    Electrodes are positioned at F3, F4, C3, C4, T7, T8, P3, and P4 to effectively capture brain activity associated with speech planning and motor control. This configuration is optimized for detecting imagined speech signals.

  • Waveshare 32-bit ADC HAT (ADS1263)

    A high-precision analog-to-digital converter offering 400 samples per second per channel across up to 10 channels. This component ensures accurate digitization of low-amplitude EEG signals, maintaining signal integrity for real-time processing.

  • Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W

    A compact and affordable microcomputer that handles real-time signal filtering, buffering, and USB-based data transmission. Ideal for on-device preprocessing in portable, wearable EEG systems.

  • Gain Settings (AD620 Instrumentation Amplifier)

    Configured for up to 1000x signal amplification using a gain resistor of approximately 49.4 Ω. Capable of amplifying microvolt-level EEG signals into readable voltages with an output swing of approximately 0.05V to 4.2V (on a 5V supply). Operates safely just below supply rails (e.g., around 13V on a ±15V supply), ensuring clean and accurate signal extraction.

Software stack

  • Real-Time Signal Conditioning

  • On-device IIR/FIR filters remove low-frequency drift, EMG artifacts, and powerline interference (50/60 Hz), ensuring clean, high-fidelity EEG signals for downstream AI processing.

  • Deep Learning Inference Engine

  • 1D CNN + LSTM + Transformer (TensorFlow/Keras)

    Hybrid neural model combining spatial pattern extraction (CNN), temporal dynamics (LSTM), and contextual attention (Transformer). Enables robust, low-latency imagined speech decoding.

    • CNN: 64 & 128 filters, ReLU activations
    • Transformer-enhanced LSTM
    • Dense layers: 128 units + Softmax output

  • Synthetic Voice Generation

  • Text-to-Speech (Coqui TTS)

    Real-time conversion of model outputs into natural, expressive speech. Open-source, lightweight, and optimized for edge deployment with high-quality audio synthesis.

What We Learnt

  • EEG Signal Acquisition

    Hands-on experience capturing low-amplitude, noise-sensitive EEG signals in real time. Explored core signal processing techniques like Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) for frequency-domain insights.

  • Advanced Filtering Techniques

    Mastered the use of bandpass and notch filters to extract clean, high-quality brain signals, ensuring reliable input for AI-based models.

  • Full-Stack Neurotech Integration

    Engineered seamless integration across analog front-ends (capacitors, op-amps), high-resolution ADCs, microcontrollers, and deep learning pipelines for real-time BCI functionality.

  • AI for Neural Decoding

    Designed and trained a CNN-LSTM hybrid model to classify EEG-based imagined speech, optimizing for both spatial and temporal features in biosignal data.

  • Robustness to Signal Variability

    Identified critical challenges such as user-specific brain patterns, electrode placement, and environmental noise—key factors impacting model generalization.

  • Human-Centered Design

    Applied design thinking to build a modular, comfortable, and aesthetically viable wearable—prioritizing ergonomics and usability.

  • Assistive Technology Impact

    Deepened our commitment to accessibility by developing intuitive, non-invasive BCI solutions aimed at enhancing communication for individuals with disabilities.

What's next ?

  • Use coaxial wires instead of twisted wires to reduce noise and improve signal clarity

  • Enable digital transmission of EEG data to preserve signal integrity
  • Enhance the LSTM model for improved word classification accuracy
  • Refine the headset's comfort, stability, and appearance for real-world usability
